About Olympia

  1. Population: 55,605
  2. Incorporated: 1859
  3. Region: Western Washington
  4. Classification: Urban
  5. Average Income Level: $73,475.00

Olympia is the capital city of Washington State and is located on the south end of the Puget Sound. Known for its vibrant arts, rich history, and beautiful waterfronts, Olympia offers a blend of cultural and natural attractions. The city offers several parks, museums, and historical landmarks.

Popular Attractions

Olympia Yacht Club

The Olympia Yacht Club, was founded in 1889 in Olympia, Washington. It is one of the oldest marinas in the Pacific Northwest. It provides members with access to South Puget Sound and offers boating events, social activities, and educational programs.

Washington State History Museum

This museum in Olympia features interactive exhibits and educational programs on Washington's history and culture, including Native American heritage and regional roles.

Imagine Children's Museum

Imagine Children's Museum interactive museum in Olympia offers hands-on exhibits for children to learn through play, covering topics like science, art, and community life. This museum is a delight for younger audiences with many locations around the Puget Sound.

Washington State Capitol

Washington State's Capitol is Olympia, a city of historic government buildings known for their colonial architecture and victorian gardens. Guided tours are available.

  • Construction: Completed in 1928 with neo-classical architecture.
  • Dome: The dome, is the tallest dome in the U.S., at 287 feet.
  • Historical Significance: Listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
  • Design: Designed by Walter R. Wilder with marble and granite interiors.
  • Legislative Sessions: Hosts annual Washington State Legislature sessions.
  • Guided Tours: Available to learn about the building and legislative process.
  • Public Access: Open grounds with monuments and gardens.
  • Artwork: It features artwork reflecting Washington's history.
  • Renovations: There have been several renovations to maintain its integrity.
  • Visitor Center: Includes many exhibits on the building's history.

Outdoor Recreation

Olympia offers outdoor activities with scenic views of water and forests. Popular spots include Percival Landing Park and Olympia Forest.

  • Percival Landing Park: Waterfront park with trails and picnic areas.
  • Olympic National Park: Features rainforests, mountains, and coastlines.
  • Water Activities: Many deal locations for kayaking, paddle boarding and sailing.
  • Hiking Trails: Features many nature trails with stunning landscape and ocean views.
  • Local Parks: Includes parks like Priest Point Park for family recreation.
  • Bird Watching: Known for its diverse bird species.
  • Fishing: Many lakes and rivers offer fishing and water spots.
  • Cycling: Paths and trails for all levels over miles of exercise.
  • Botanical Gardens: Showcases local some of the states best flora exhibits.
  • Seasonal Events: Outdoor festivals celebrating the region's beauty and culture.
